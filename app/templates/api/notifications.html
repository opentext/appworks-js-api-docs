AppworksJS gives you access to native notifications intended for your target app.

Enabling Notifications

Notifications are enabled by default. This means that any new notification gets stored by appworksJS and is made available through the api.

To enable notifications:

    appworks.notifications.on => ()

Receiving Notifications

There are two ways to get notifications. First, you may ask for all of the notifications available to your target app. This will be returned to you in an array. Second, you may ask for notifications that come in while the app is open. To do this, you must register a callback using the handler() method. Your callback will be fired whenever a new notification is received by the app, with the notification contents as argument.

Receiving all notifications

    appworks.notifications.get => ()

Receiving new notifications

    appworks.notifications.handler => (callback) 
    
        callback: a function to be called whenever a new notification is received by the app. argument sent to this
        callback is a json representation of the notification.

Clear Notifications

You may clear the notifications queue by issuing a call to clear(). This will remove all notifications. New notifications may still populate the list unless notifications are explicitly disabled. 

    appworks.notifications.clear => ()
